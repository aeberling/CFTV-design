üí° Why These Changes?

1. Tin Cup Serves Two Audiences
Donors see "Become a Tin Cup Challenger" as a tertiary page under Give ‚Üí Ways to Give. Nonprofits see "Tin Cup Challenge" with Program Schedule under Grants & Scholarships. Same event, different entry points based on user intent.
2. Start a Fund is About Giving
"Start a Fund" now lives under Give where donors can easily find it. The page consolidates Fund Types & Structures, Donor-Advised, Memorial Funds, Scholarships, and Agency Funds in one place.
3. "Get Involved" is Community-Focused
"Get Involved" is for community engagement: Nonprofit Directory, Newsgroup/Listserv, Volunteer, Nonprofit Jobs, and Become a Challenger. Operation Independence moved to About as it's more about the foundation's programs.
4. Ways to Give Has Tertiary Pages
"Ways to Give" is now a hub page with tertiary pages: Give to the Community Foundation, Become a Tin Cup Challenger, and Planned Giving. This provides a clear hierarchy for donor-focused content.
5. Streamlined About Section
About now contains: Our Mission, Impact Stories, Meet Our Team (staff + board combined), Annual Reports, Strategic Plan, and Operation Independence. News & Events and Contact moved to utility bar.
6. Apply for Grants Hub
Nonprofits seeking funding have a clear path: For Nonprofits ‚Üí Apply for Grants, which links to Youth Philanthropy, Competitive Grants, Tin Cup Challenge, and Opportunity Fund.
