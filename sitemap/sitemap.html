üí° Why These Changes?

1. Tin Cup Belongs With Giving
The Tin Cup Challenge is your flagship fundraising event where people give money to nonprofits. It was misplaced under "Grants & Scholarships" (where money goes out). Moving it to "Give" creates a logical flow: Want to donate? ‚Üí Give ‚Üí Tin Cup Challenge.
2. Fund Administration ‚â† Giving Methods
"Ways to Give" had 10 items mixing donation methods with fund management tools. A first-time donor doesn't need "Fund Management" or "Funding Portal"‚Äîthose are for existing fund holders. Separated these into "Start a Fund" and "Manage Your Fund" under Grants & Scholarships.
3. "Get Involved" is Action-Oriented
"Community Impact" was passive and mixed news with engagement. "Get Involved" tells visitors exactly what they can do: find jobs, volunteer, become a challenger, read impact stories. Foundation news moves to "About" where organizational info belongs.
4. Clear Labels Reduce Confusion
"Messaging" ‚Üí "Newsgroup / Listserv" tells users exactly what it is. "Nonprofit Workshops" ‚Üí "Workshops & Training" is more comprehensive. "Join Challengers" ‚Üí "Become a Challenger" is more inviting.
5. Every Page Has One Home
No more duplicate Tin Cup or News links. Each page appears in exactly one place. Cross-references (like "Become a Challenger" appearing in both Give and Get Involved) link to the same destination.
